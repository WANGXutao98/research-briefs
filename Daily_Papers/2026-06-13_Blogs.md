- **URL**: https://openai.com/index/using-codex-to-simulate-black-holes/

**Summary**: OpenAI profiled researcher Chi-kwan Chan at the University of Arizona's Steward Observatory, who uses Codex to iteratively refine and test algorithms that simulate the movement of electrons and ions around black holes. Chan's work addresses fundamental limits in current algorithms and available compute that restrict the realism of black-hole simulations, one of the best testbeds for Einstein's general theory of relativity. Codex accelerates the write-test-debug cycle for computationally intensive simulation code, allowing Chan to explore more algorithm variants per unit time. OpenAI also published a companion demo video showing the simulation workflow in action.

**Research Inspiration**: This is a concrete existence proof that AI code agents can meaningfully accelerate physics simulation development — the same pattern applies to world models for embodied AI, where writing, testing, and tuning differentiable physics simulators (e.g., in Isaac Sim or MuJoCo) is a major bottleneck. ## Google DeepMind

### From AGI to ASI
- **Date**: 2026-06-10 (ArXiv preprint) / ~2026-06-12 (blog)
- **URL**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.12683
- **Blog**: https://deepmind.google/blog/

**Summary**: Google DeepMind published a 60-page technical roadmap examining how artificial general intelligence could transition into artificial superintelligence. The paper identifies four non-mutually-exclusive pathways to ASI: (1) continued scaling of compute, data, and model size; (2) new algorithms and architectural innovations beyond current paradigms; (3) recursive self-improvement, where models iteratively improve their own training or architecture; and (4) multi-agent collectives, where networks of AGI-level agents achieve emergent collective intelligence exceeding any individual agent. The paper situates itself in a deliberate research program following DeepMind's March 2026 cognitive framework paper and their April 2025 AGI safety work — first define AGI, then make it safe, then map the path beyond it. The four pathways are analyzed for feasibility timelines, safety challenges, and prerequisites.

**Research Inspiration**: The multi-agent collectives pathway is directly relevant to embodied AI — if ASI emerges from fleets of coordinated agents, then architectures for multi-robot systems with shared world models become foundational infrastructure for the ASI transition, not just engineering conveniences. The recursive self-improvement pathway also has implications for world model research: a world model that can generate its own training data (via imagination/rollout) and refine its own architecture through self-evaluation is a form of recursive self-improvement within a bounded domain. This paper provides conceptual scaffolding for framing world model research in a longer-horizon context.

## NVIDIA

### NVIDIA Unveils New Open Models, Data and Tools to Advance AI Across Every Industry
- **Date**: 2026-06-12
- **URL**: https://blogs.nvidia.com/blog/open-models-data-tools-accelerate-ai/
- **Press release**: https://nvidianews.nvidia.com/news/nvidia-releases-new-physical-ai-models-as-global-partners-unveil-next-generation-robots

**Summary**: NVIDIA released a comprehensive suite of open physical AI models targeting robotics, autonomous vehicles, and multimodal understanding. The flagship releases are **Cosmos Predict 2.5** (unifying three prior models into one, generating 30-second videos from a single frame for rapid world simulation), **Cosmos Transfer 2.5** (photorealistic data synthesis from 3D scenes at one-third the compute of Cosmos Transfer 1), **Cosmos Reason 2** (a reasoning vision-language model available as an NVIDIA NIM microservice), and **Isaac GR00T N1.6** (an open reasoning VLA model purpose-built for humanoid robots). GR00T N1.6 uses a variant of the Cosmos-Reason-2B VLM with native resolution support, a 2× larger diffusion transformer (32 layers) for smoother motion control, and state-relative action predictions for better positional generalization. The model was trained on thousands of hours of diverse teleoperation data spanning humanoids, mobile manipulators, and bimanual arms to enable cross-embodiment generalization. NVIDIA also released open training data: 10 trillion language tokens, 500,000 robotics trajectories, 455,000 protein structures, and 100 TB of vehicle sensor data.

**Key Technical Finding**: Isaac GR00T N1.6's integration of Cosmos-Reason-2B as the perception backbone achieves native resolution understanding (no distortion from resizing), which translates to better scene understanding and more reliable task decomposition for dexterous manipulation tasks.

**Research Inspiration**: This is the highest-relevance release of the scan for world model and embodied AI research. Cosmos Predict 2.5 generating 30-second physically plausible videos from a single frame is essentially an open-source world model for physical AI — understanding its architecture and failure modes should be a near-term research priority. The 500,000 robotics trajectories open dataset lowers the barrier to training embodied models significantly. GR00T N1.6's sim-to-real workflow using Cosmos for data augmentation is a concrete, reproducible pipeline for scaling robot learning. Key questions for follow-up: what are GR00T N1.6's out-of-distribution generalization limits, and how does Cosmos Predict 2.5 handle long-horizon physical consistency vs. prior diffusion-based world models?
