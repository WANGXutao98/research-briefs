- **Date**: 2026-06-04 *(just outside 48-hour window; included for relevance)*
- **URL**: https://www.anthropic.com/news/AI-enabled-cyber-threats-mitre-attack

**Summary**: Anthropic analyzed 832 banned accounts engaged in malicious cyber activity from March 2025 to March 2026, mapping their behaviors to the MITRE ATT&CK framework — recording 13,873 actions across 482 unique ATT&CK techniques. Key finding: AI is democratizing advanced attack capabilities; the share of medium-or-higher-risk actors rose from 33% to 56% over the year. 67.3% of banned actors used AI for malware authoring; 6.5% used it for lateral movement. Crucially, the most dangerous agentic behaviors (orchestrating multi-step attack chains autonomously) are not yet captured in existing ATT&CK taxonomy — Anthropic is collaborating with MITRE to extend the framework.

**Research Inspiration**: The finding that AI-driven agentic behavior poses novel, unclassified threats is directly analogous to challenges in embodied AI safety. For world model research, this highlights the importance of modeling agent intention and action sequences — the same capability that enables useful long-horizon robot planning can enable harmful autonomous cyber operations. Safe agentic world models may need to include adversarial scenario modeling in their training distributions.

## Google DeepMind

### From AGI to ASI
- **Date**: 2026-06-12
- **URL**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.12683

**Summary**: A 60-page roadmap from Google DeepMind researchers (led by Tim Genewein, co-authored by Shane Legg and Marcus Hutter) formally maps four non-mutually-exclusive pathways from AGI (defined as median-human cognitive performance across most tasks) to ASI (defined as surpassing large, coordinated expert teams across virtually all domains). The four routes are: (1) **Scaling** — continued increases in compute, data, and model size; (2) **Algorithmic paradigm shifts** — fundamentally new learning or reasoning methods; (3) **Recursive self-improvement** — AGI-level systems modifying and improving themselves; (4) **Multi-agent collectives** — networks of AGI agents whose combined intelligence exceeds individual capability. The paper also characterizes hard limits and bottlenecks at each transition, including alignment and coordination challenges within agent collectives.

**Research Inspiration**: The multi-agent collective pathway is directly relevant to embodied AI: swarms of embodied agents with shared or distributed world models could constitute an early form of collective superintelligence. For world model research, this paper motivates studying how to compose world models across agents — particularly how individual learned environment models can be shared, updated, and reconciled in multi-agent settings. The recursive self-improvement pathway also suggests value in studying world models that can be refined by the agents that use them (analogous to model-based RL with self-generated experience).

## NVIDIA Research

### GraspGen-X: Cross-Embodiment 6-DOF Diffusion-based Grasping
- **Date**: 2026-06 (arXiv: 2606.00998; presented at ICRA 2026)
- **URL**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.00998
- **Project**: https://graspgenx.github.io/

**Summary**: NVIDIA researchers (Beining Han, Yu-Wei Chao, Erwin Coumans, Clemens Eppner, Jia Deng, Stan Birchfield, Adithyavairavan Murali) extend the GraspGen diffusion-transformer framework to cross-embodiment grasping. The key innovation is representing each gripper by its **Swept Volume** — the 3D region traced by fingers during a grasping motion — as a compact, morphology-agnostic input to both the generator and discriminator. Trained on 2 billion simulated grasps across 25 procedurally generated grippers (parallel 2-finger, revolute 2-finger, high-DOF 3-finger), GraspGen-X achieves **zero-shot generalization to novel gripper morphologies** never seen in training, and serves as a strong finetuning initialization for new target grippers. Real-world transfer without domain adaptation demonstrates strong robustness to visual noise and partial observations.

**Research Inspiration**: The Swept Volume gripper representation is an elegant solution to the embodiment gap: rather than learning gripper-specific policies, learn a geometry-aware manipulation prior that generalizes. For embodied AI and world models, this suggests a complementary strategy — encoding object-agent interaction geometry into the world model's latent space so that the model can generalize manipulation predictions across robot morphologies. A world model trained with swept-volume-conditioned action representations could enable zero-shot transfer of manipulation knowledge to new robot bodies.

### ScheduleStream: Temporal Planning with Samplers for GPU-Accelerated Multi-Arm Task and Motion Planning & Scheduling
- **Date**: 2026-06-01 (ICRA 2026)
- **URL**: https://research.nvidia.com/publication/2026-06_schedulestream-temporal-planning-samplers-gpu-accelerated-multi-arm-task-and

**Summary**: NVIDIA researchers (Caelan Garrett and Fabio Ramos) present ScheduleStream, a framework for GPU-accelerated multi-arm task and motion planning that integrates temporal planning with learned samplers. The system enables coordinated multi-arm scheduling by treating collision-free trajectory generation as a streaming sampling problem, leveraging GPU parallelism to dramatically reduce planning time for complex manipulation sequences. Presented at ICRA 2026.

**Research Inspiration**: Multi-arm coordination under temporal constraints is a compelling test bed for world models: the model must simultaneously maintain representations of multiple end-effectors, their joint states, and shared object state over extended horizons. ScheduleStream's sampler-based approach is compatible with model-based planning — a learned world model could serve as the environment dynamics oracle within the sampler, enabling gradient-guided or model-predictive trajectory generation for multi-arm systems.
