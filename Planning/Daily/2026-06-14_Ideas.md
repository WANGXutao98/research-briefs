## 1. New Research Questions (Emerged Today)

### Question 1: Can Geometry-Aware Representations Make World Models Physically Grounded by Design?

**The Question**: NVIDIA's GraspGen-X (ICRA 2026) represents each gripper by its **Swept Volume** — the 3D region traced by fingers during grasping — enabling zero-shot transfer across gripper morphologies never seen in training. Current video world models (Cosmos 3, BiWM, AHEAD, AHA-WAM) represent the world as pixel or patch-feature sequences, with no geometric encoding of how tools interact with objects. The question: can encoding **object-action interaction geometry** (the swept volume of end-effectors, the contact surface of grasped objects, the trajectory of rigid bodies) into a world model's latent space produce predictions that are physically consistent by construction, rather than by post-hoc physical grounding?

**Why It Matters Now**: GraspGen-X demonstrates that geometry-aware encoding solves zero-shot embodiment generalization for grasping (a single fixed skill). If extended to a world model's full prediction space, the same principle could solve physical consistency without requiring a correction module — eliminating the need for the failure taxonomy + post-hoc correction approach (Idea 2 from yesterday's report) entirely. This is a more principled solution than retrofitting physics into a pixel model.

**Why It's Tractable**: The Swept Volume concept can be approximated in a world model by augmenting input state with: (a) a per-timestep 3D bounding box of the active end-effector trajectory, (b) a contact surface map between tool and object (from MuJoCo's contact API, which is freely available). These can serve as additional conditioning channels for AHEAD's feature-space world model without changing its architecture. A 2-week ablation comparing pixel-only vs. geometry-augmented conditioning on peg insertion tasks is feasible on H100x1.

**Evidence**: GraspGen-X (arxiv 2606.00998, NVIDIA ICRA 2026, graspgenx.github.io), AHEAD (arXiv 2606.02486), Cosmos 3 (arXiv 2606.02800).

### Question 2: Does Spatially Adaptive Computation (Foveation) Improve World Model Rollout Quality for Robot Control?

**The Question**: `bchao1/foveated_diffusion` (GitHub, ★8, appeared twice in consecutive GitHub scans — June 13 and 14 — indicating growing community traction) introduces spatially adaptive compute allocation to diffusion models: high-resolution generation in attended regions, low-resolution in periphery. For video world models used in robot control, the attended region should be action-relevant: the gripper, target object, contact zone. The question: does foveated generation — computing high-fidelity predictions only in action-relevant spatial regions while compressing background — improve the policy-relevant information content of world model rollouts, and reduce rollout compute enough to enable real-time closed-loop control?

**Why It Matters Now**: Efficient-WAM and Flash-WAM both attack the compute bottleneck via temporal/denoising-step compression. Foveated Diffusion attacks it spatially. Crucially, spatial compression that concentrates compute on action-relevant regions is not just an efficiency trick — it may actually improve policy utility by removing distracting background information that currently drowns out the action-relevant signal in patch-feature world model representations like AHEAD.

**Why It's Tractable**: The foveated_diffusion codebase is open. AHEAD's feature-space world model operates on visual patch tokens — implementing foveation requires only a spatial attention mask that suppresses patch update computation outside a defined ROI (end-effector bounding box + object ROI from an off-the-shelf object detector). This is a ≤2-week modification. Evaluation: compare WMATS score of foveated vs. standard AHEAD rollouts on tabletop pushing/peg insertion.

**Evidence**: `bchao1/foveated_diffusion` (GitHub, ★8, June 9–14 2026), Efficient-WAM (arXiv 2606.10040), AHA-WAM (arXiv 2606.09811), AHEAD (arXiv 2606.02486).

### Question 3: With Five WAM Papers in One Week, Which WAM Architecture Actually Matters for Policy Learning?

**The Question**: Between June 8 and June 12, five distinct WAM architectures appeared: AHEAD (latent-space motion prediction, 4.9M params), Efficient-WAM (token-sparse + asymmetric denoising), AHA-WAM (dual-DiT temporal asymmetry), Flash-WAM (modality-aware step distillation), and Cosmos 3 (omnimodal MoE unification). Each claims superior performance on video generation metrics (FVD, SSIM) or inference speed. But **not one of these papers measures downstream policy success rate improvement attributable to the world model** — the exact measurement WMATS is designed to provide. The question is now sharpened: across these five architectures, which produces the largest policy utility gain, and does that rank correlate at all with their video generation benchmark rankings?

**Why It Matters Now**: This is the most favorable timing possible for the WMATS paper. Five WAM architectures exist, are open-source, are benchmarked on video metrics, and have **zero downstream policy evaluations**. A paper that runs all five through a standardized policy-utility protocol is definitional — it becomes the canonical ranking that every future WAM paper must cite or challenge. The value of the benchmark grows quadratically with the number of WAMs it can evaluate.

**Why It's Tractable**: AHEAD (4.9M extra params on a frozen 7B backbone) is the easiest entry point for the no-WM vs. WM comparison because its architecture explicitly separates the policy backbone from the world model augmentation. The 5-architecture sweep can be run on H100x8 over 3-4 days once the protocol is established. Start with AHEAD vs. no-WM baseline (H100x1, 2 days), then scale.

**Evidence**: AHEAD (arXiv 2606.02486), Efficient-WAM (arXiv 2606.10040), AHA-WAM (arXiv 2606.09811), Flash-WAM (arXiv 2606.05254), Cosmos 3 (arXiv 2606.02800), WorldOlympiad (arXiv 2606.11129), `tigee1311/worldbench` (GitHub June 10).

### Idea 2: Geometry-Augmented World Models — Encoding Action-Object Interaction for Physical Consistency

- **Core Hypothesis**: Video world models fail on contact-rich tasks because pixel sequences cannot represent the causal geometry of tool-object interaction — they see what happens but not *why* it happens. Augmenting the world model's conditioning with a lightweight geometry channel (swept-volume mask of the end-effector trajectory + object contact surface map, both computable from simulator state) causes the model to internalize physical causality, reducing contact-discontinuity prediction errors by ≥30% relative to pixel-only models on peg insertion benchmarks.

  - **What's new**: First paper to apply geometry-aware encoding (contact surfaces, swept volumes) as conditioning signals for a video world model, closing the gap between GraspGen-X's embodiment-generalization insight and world model physical consistency.

### Idea 3: Foveated World Models — Action-Relevant Spatial Attention for Efficient and Policy-Useful Rollouts

- **One-liner**: A spatially adaptive world model rollout mechanism that concentrates generation compute on action-relevant regions (end-effector, target object, contact zone), reducing rollout cost while improving WMATS by suppressing task-irrelevant background noise in the policy training signal.
- **Core Hypothesis**: The noise introduced into policy training by generating high-fidelity predictions for task-irrelevant scene regions (backgrounds, off-scene objects) is a measurable liability, not a neutral overhead; a foveated world model that suppresses non-ROI regions during rollout improves WMATS relative to a full-scene world model with equal compute budget, because the policy receives cleaner action-relevant signals.
- **Novelty vs Existing Work**:
  - Foveated Diffusion (bchao1/foveated_diffusion, GitHub June 9): spatially adaptive generation for *image/video synthesis quality* — not applied to world model policy utility.
  - AHEAD (arXiv 2606.02486): uniform patch-feature prediction, no spatial attention.
  - Efficient-WAM (arXiv 2606.10040): token-sparse latents (structured sparsity) vs. spatial foveation — different mechanism.
  - **What's new**: First application of foveated spatial compute to world model rollouts with explicit ROI defined by action relevance (end-effector/object bounding box from simulator), evaluated on policy utility rather than generation fidelity.
- **Expected Contribution**: [ ] Theory [x] Method [ ] Benchmark [ ] System
- **Feasibility**:
  - Implementation: Add a spatial mask to AHEAD's patch attention that down-weights patches outside EE bounding box + 20% margin. The mask is dynamic per rollout step. ≤2 weeks implementation.
  - Evaluation: Report both FVD (expect decrease because background is compressed) and WMATS (expect increase if hypothesis holds). The double movement — FVD down, WMATS up — directly supports the WMATS paper's core claim that FVD and policy utility are decorrelated.

## 5. Open Research Gaps (High-Opportunity Zones)

- **Gap: Policy Utility Measurement — The Biggest Missing Metric in World Model Research**
  Five WAM papers dropped in one week. All five are benchmarked on video quality (FVD, PSNR, WorldOlympiad scores). Zero are benchmarked on downstream policy success rate improvement attributable to the world model. `tigee1311/worldbench` (GitHub June 10) enters the prediction-consistency sub-space but not the policy outcome space. This gap is now *more* open with each new WAM paper, because every new WAM that lacks a policy evaluation extends the scope of what WMATS-Bench will cover.
  **Evidence**: AHEAD (arXiv 2606.02486 — first WAM to report real-robot task success; 29/30 vs. 0/30 for baselines), WorldOlympiad (arXiv 2606.11129 — no policy loop), all 5 WAMs described above.
  **Entry Point**: The WMATS anchor experiment (no-WM Diffusion Policy baseline, 10K steps, MuJoCo peg insertion). Commit the 300-word design doc today, start the experiment tomorrow.

- **Gap: Tactile/Proprioceptive World Models for Contact-Rich Tasks**
  Every world model this week is vision-centric. The NVIDIA Physical AI Dataset (500K trajectories, open, 4.8M+ downloads) includes force/torque logs that no published paper uses for world model training. Contact-rich manipulation (peg insertion, gear assembly) is fundamentally a tactile task — visual fidelity is insufficient for predicting whether contact succeeded. This gap is large and the data exists to fill it.
  **Evidence**: NVIDIA Physical AI Dataset (force/torque logs, open on Hugging Face), GR00T N1.6 focus on dexterous manipulation (NVIDIA blog June 12), absence of any proprioceptive branch in AHEAD/BiWM/Cosmos 3.
  **Entry Point**: Extend AHEAD's feature-space world model with a proprioceptive prediction branch (predict next joint torques and EE force from current RGB + current joint state). Train on a subset of the Physical AI Dataset where force labels exist. Evaluate on peg insertion at <1mm tolerance — if the proprioceptive branch reduces task success variance, the modality matters.

- **Gap: Long-Horizon Physical Consistency Beyond 30 Seconds**
  Cosmos Predict 2.5 generates 30-second videos from a single frame — representing the current frontier. Long-horizon robot tasks (assembly, meal prep, lab automation) operate on 5-10 minute horizons. The WorldOlympiad interactive track tests controllability but not over extended durations. No current world model claims physical consistency beyond 30 seconds, and no benchmark measures it. State-relative prediction (predict Δ-state, not next-state) is a principled approach to suppressing drift in long-horizon rollouts.
  **Evidence**: Cosmos Predict 2.5 (NVIDIA, June 12 2026), WorldOlympiad (arXiv 2606.11129), AHA-WAM (arXiv 2606.09811 — temporal asymmetry motivation implicitly assumes sub-30s horizons).
  **Entry Point**: Generate Cosmos Predict 2.5 rollouts for a 60-second scenario (2× its reported horizon) and measure physical consistency decay. If the model fails systematically at a specific failure mode (usually contact discontinuities or object permanence violations), identify the failure signature and propose state-relative prediction as a fix.

- **Gap: Speech/Instruction Robustness for World-Model-Conditioned Policies**
  Parley (GitHub, June 9) benchmarks how ASR noise propagates through *VLA* pipelines but not through *world-model-conditioned* pipelines. If a world model is conditioned on a noisy/truncated language instruction, do its rollouts degrade gracefully or catastrophically? This is unexplored — every WAM paper assumes clean text instructions. For real-world deployment, this gap is safety-critical.
  **Evidence**: Parley (psychopathdev/parley, GitHub June 9), VICX (arXiv 2606.12028 — language-conditioned visual plans), AHEAD (language conditioning not studied).
  **Entry Point**: Run AHEAD's world model with perturbed language instructions (using Parley's perturbation toolkit) and measure rollout prediction quality degradation relative to clean instruction baselines. This is a 1-week experiment that could expand into a robustness paper or a section of the WMATS paper.
